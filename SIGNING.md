# Signing the SHA-4 Initiative open letter

Individuals can sign by pull request or by email. Organisations sign by email only — we confirm via a reply to an email on the organisation's own domain before publishing, and that step does not fit cleanly into a pull request.

## Individuals

### By pull request

Edit [`_data/signatories.yml`](_data/signatories.yml) and append to the `individuals` list:

    - name: Jane Smith
      affiliation: Independent researcher   # optional; free-text, e.g. "ETH Zürich" or "Independent researcher"
      link: https://example.com/janesmith   # required; a public profile — Scholar, ORCID, GitHub, X/Mastodon, institutional page, or personal site

Then open a pull request.

### By email

If you prefer not to use GitHub, email [want@sha4.info](mailto:want@sha4.info) with:

- Your name
- Affiliation (optional — "Independent researcher" is fine)
- A public profile link (Scholar, ORCID, GitHub, X/Mastodon, institutional page, or personal site)

## Organisations

Email [want@sha4.info](mailto:want@sha4.info) from an address on the organisation's own domain, and include:

- Organisation name
- Name and role of the person signing on its behalf
- Organisation website

We will reply from the same channel to confirm before publishing.

## Review

Signatories are reviewed before publication:

- **Individual signatures.** We check that the public profile you provide matches the name and is not obviously fabricated. If you claim an affiliation, we may cross-check against the institution's public pages.
- **Organisational signatures.** We confirm via reply to an email on the organisation's own domain.

We publish in batches. If we have questions, we will reply on your pull request or to the email address you signed from.
